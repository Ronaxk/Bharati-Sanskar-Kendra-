About Us -

Bharati Sanskar Kendra was formally established in 2010. ‘Bharati’ means Goddess Sarasvati, the Hindu goddess of knowledge, art, music, learning and wisdom. ‘Sanskar’ means all the activities to bring quality, purity and stability in life alternatively ‘Sanskar’ means refinement to achieve perfection which will enable a person to lead a life of utility to society. ‘Kendra’ means the place or center. True to the name, our vision is to bring a sustainable change in the lives of poor people by empowering women and children in rural areas. The main emphasis is on improvement of their health, imparting quality education and empowering the women with skill development. We conduct programs and activities such as • Run support classes for poor children. • Conduct various skill trainings for women. • Science projects by using trash. • Reduce and Reuse” plastic. • Eco club Zilha parishad schools. • Computer education poor children. • Health check-up for children and ladies. • Celebration of nutrition month.

Our Beginning

In 2007, a huge construction work was going on near my house, about 15 small children of diffent age groups between 3 to 11 years used to daily go with their construction labour parents in the morning and whole day they use to play in mud and inhaling cement dust,some times they got hurt by construction material like nails,iron rods etc.which was lying near by, this made me very uneasy and one day I asked them why don't you go to school? They said that they don't understand Marathi,they come from M.P. , I asked If I teach them in hindi are they ready to come? They immediately replied yes,from very next day they started coming and I started to teach them in my verandah, initially I and my 7years old son started this work. After some time more children joined this informal school some volunteers also joined us. After some time we realised many more different problems in near by brick kiln and our work areas expanded and then in 2010 we finally decided to register our Organization Bharati Sanskar Kendra.Bharati Sanskar Kendra was formally established in 2010. ‘Bharati’ means Goddess Sarasvati, the Hindu goddess of knowledge, art, music, learning and wisdom. ‘Sanskar’ means all the activities to bring quality, purity and stability in life alternatively ‘Sanskar’ means refinement to achieve perfection which will enable a person to lead a life of utility to society. ‘Kendra’ means the place or center. Our Vision

Our Vision is to bring a sustainable change in the lives of poor people, especially women and children, living in rural areas. Our main emphasis is on improvement of their health conditions, education and women empowerment through skill development leading to income generation! An efficient, effective and sustainable charitable sector.To efficiently and effectively regulate and enable the charitable sector to enhance its role in National development.Our Mission is to provide following to the women and children of rural areas. - Help in imparting quality education to underprivileged children - Improve health status of women and children - Conduct women related skill development programs leading to employment generation We aim to implement this by ushering in awareness about various government programs which will uplift their socio-economic status. We also aim to collaborate with government, corporates and other NGOs in implementing various programs in consonance with our vision.

Stories

We are working with an objective to improve environment, health, education and econimic status of rural women and children.

Environment

Fruits tree plantation in different anganwadis. Prepare more than 4000 seed balls every year with help of Z.p. school children and throw it before rainy season. Develop nutrition gardens at the anganwadi centres. Installing vermicompost unit at various places.Installing zero energy cool chamber.

Health

Conducted various health awareness exhibitions. Conduct various health camps such as Dental checkup, dyslexia, haemoglobin level, blood sugar, blood pressure, breast Cancer etc. Conducted need based Nutrition education programs. Conducted Fruit and Vegetable preservation training.

Education

Our Organisation try to improve the level of village women by providing non formal education. Conducted various skill training according to their interest. Trainings conducted till now are: Gravy premix making, Soap making, Candle making, Cake premix making, Cake making, Cloth bags making, School bags making, Basic garments stitching.

Nutrition Education

The Nutrition Education Program delivers evidence-based nutrition education and obesity prevention interventions through a combination of education strategies to promote healthy eating and active lifestyles.

Environment

Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program Education (SNAP-Ed) is an evidence-based program that helps people lead healthy, active lives. SNAP-Ed teaches people how to make their SNAP dollars stretch, how to shop for and cook healthy meals, and how to stay physically active.

Environment

The Expanded Food and Nutrition Education Program (EFNEP) uses education to support participants' efforts toward self-sufficiency, nutritional health, and well-being. EFNEP combines hands-on learning, applied science, and program data to ensure program effectiveness, efficiency, and accountability.
